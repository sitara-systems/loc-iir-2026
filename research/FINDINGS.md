## Unexpected Finds

1. **El Tucsonense (Tucson, AZ)** — 398 hits on railroad worker keywords, publishing continuously 1915–1935+ in Spanish, covering labor conditions, repatriation, and railroad work from within the affected community. Not anticipated as a primary source.

2. **La opinión (Los Angeles)** — dense June 1929 coverage of California deportaciones, right at the onset. Multiple issues in a single week document the beginning of the forced removals in real time.

3. **Dillingham Commission "Dictionary of races or peoples" (1910)** — explicitly classifies "Hindus" and South Asian groups for immigration purposes. This is the bureaucratic framework that would have categorized Mohaiyuddin Khan's identity in federal records.

4. **The absence of Mohaiyuddin Khan in all LC searches** — negative result is itself evidence. With 32 searches across multiple collection types, including a direct name search, the person does not appear in any LC-indexed material. This confirms the "hidden in plain sight" thesis: the story exists in federal archives (NARA naturalization files) but is completely invisible through current LC discovery tools.

5. **Carbon dioxide press silence 1960-1975** — 1 newspaper result in 15 years confirms that public discourse and scientific knowledge were operating in completely separate channels.
